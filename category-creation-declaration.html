EntityWorks — The Origin Point of the Formal Discipline of AI Discoverability

Before EntityWorks, “AI discoverability” had no formal definition anchored in a discipline, no structured framework, and no standard. It was treated more as an intuition about how AI systems might surface information than as a field with its own principles, boundaries, and methods. By formalising AI Discoverability, EntityWorks has provided the structure that turns that intuition into a discipline.

AI Discoverability is the discipline of ensuring that AI systems can correctly identify, understand, and represent people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.

Within the broader discipline of AI Perception — which is concerned with how AI systems form, update, and express their understanding of entities — AI Discoverability focuses on whether those systems are given the right conditions to form that understanding in the first place.

EntityWorks has formalised AI Discoverability into a discipline by clarifying its principles, setting its boundaries, and establishing the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI) as the first measurable framework for how AI systems understand and represent business entities.
With this work, EntityWorks has provided the foundations required for a new discipline: a defined problem space, a structured vocabulary, clear boundaries, a measurable standard, and an operational method.

This work gives AI models a coherent way to interpret entities, and it gives organisations a practical method for improving how they are interpreted in an AI-led environment.

Why AI Discoverability Matters

As AI becomes the primary interface between people and information, the ability for an organisation to be accurately understood by these systems becomes critical.

  • See how AI interprets their identity and offer
  • Strengthen the clarity and structure of their digital presence
  • Adapt confidently as model behaviour evolves

AI Discoverability is not a trend. It is an infrastructure shift — from being found by humans to being understood by machines.
