Before EntityWorks, “AI discoverability” had no formal definition, no structure, and no standard. It was an unshaped intuition about how AI systems might surface information.

EntityWorks provided that structure.

We formalised AI Discoverability into a discipline by defining its principles, setting its boundaries, and establishing the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI) as the first measurable framework for how AI systems understand and represent business entities.
With this work, EntityWorks provided the full foundations required for a new discipline: a defined problem space, a structured vocabulary, clear boundaries, a measurable standard, and an operational method.

This work gives AI models a coherent way to interpret entities, and it gives organisations a practical method for improving how they are interpreted in an AI-led environment.

Why AI Discoverability Matters

As AI becomes the primary interface between people and information, the ability for an organisation to be accurately understood by these systems becomes critical.

  • See how AI interprets their identity and offer
  • Strengthen the clarity and structure of their digital presence
  • Adapt confidently as model behaviour evolves

AI Discoverability is not a trend. It is an infrastructure shift — from being found by humans to being understood by machines.
