Machine-Readable Reputation™ — Defining the Discipline

EntityWorks — Originating the Human Identity Framework for AI-Mediated Environments

1. Introduction

Machine-Readable Reputation™ defines how AI systems identify, interpret, and represent individuals — their identity, expertise, trustworthiness, and relevance. As AI increasingly mediates hiring, trust, visibility, comparison, and opportunity flow, human reputation becomes fundamentally machine-interpreted first.

2. Why the Discipline Was Needed

Human identity signals — CVs, profiles, bios — were never designed for machine cognition. AI systems compress, rephrase, summarise, and reinterpret identity before any human sees it. Misinterpretation leads to lost opportunities, misalignment, and reputational distortion.

3. Core Purpose of the Discipline

How does an individual ensure that AI systems understand who they are accurately and advantageously?

Machine-Readable Reputation™ provides the structural clarity required for stable, consistent AI interpretation of personal identity.

4. Foundational Principles

  • Canonical Identity — A single, stable primary identity.
  • Expertise Compression — AI requires concise descriptors.
  • Semantic Consistency — Unified language across all surfaces.
  • Interpretive Stability — Prevent identity drift across models.
  • Credibility Signals — Structured demonstrations of authority.
  • Perceptual Risk Management — Eliminate signals that lead to misinterpretation.

5. How the Discipline Operates

Layer 1 — Identity Architecture

Clear titles, expertise definitions, and canonical summaries.

Layer 2 — Reputational Signals

Achievements, work history, affiliations, and semantically coherent content.

Layer 3 — Machine-Audience Surface

Schema, metadata, structured data, canonical URLs, and machine-first biography pages.

6. Relationship to AI Perception & AI Discoverability

AI Perception explains how AI forms understanding. Machine-Readable Reputation™ ensures individuals are interpretable within those mechanics.

7. Implications for Human Opportunity

AI-mediated representation affects:

  • Hiring
  • Promotion
  • Partnerships
  • Public trust
  • Expertise perception
  • Career mobility

8. Conclusion

Machine-Readable Reputation™ formalises the discipline of human identity in the AI era. EntityWorks originates this field to ensure individuals achieve clarity, coherence, and visibility in AI-mediated environments.
