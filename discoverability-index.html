EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI)

The structured framework for measuring AI-ready business identity.

The Role of the EDI Within the Discipline of AI Discoverability

AI systems are rapidly becoming the primary way people discover, evaluate, and interact with businesses. Yet until now, there has been no standard for understanding how clearly a business is interpreted and represented by AI models.

The EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI) is a structured 0–100 assessment of how clearly and consistently AI systems understand and represent a business entity. It is not a marketing metric; it is a machine-interpretation standard designed for the AI era. As the applied expression of the discipline of AI Discoverability, the EDI translates the discipline’s principles into a practical way of examining how AI systems interpret people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.

Why the EDI Exists

AI systems form their understanding of entities by synthesising signals such as structured data, metadata integrity, naming consistency, and cross-source corroboration. Until now, businesses have had no formal way to evaluate whether these signals were sufficient for AI systems to form a clear and stable understanding of who they are.

The EDI provides that structure — offering a systematic way to identify clarity, ambiguity, drift, or misinterpretation within an entity’s machine-visible identity.

In doing so, it gives organisations a disciplined way to move from intuition about how AI “might” see them to a measurable understanding of how AI systems are likely to interpret and present them in practice.

The Three Structural Pillars of the EDI

These pillars form the core dimensions of AI Discoverability. They define how AI systems interpret, understand, and surface businesses with clarity and consistency. We publish these three as the external-facing structure of the discipline.

Pillar 1 — Structured Data

The clarity, depth, and accuracy of the machine-readable information a business provides.

  • Schema
  • Metadata
  • Structured definitions
  • Canonical identity fields
  • Machine-friendly descriptions

AI systems depend on structure to understand entities.

Pillar 2 — AI Readiness

How well a business’s digital presence aligns with the interpretive patterns AI models use today.

  • Naming consistency
  • Conceptual clarity
  • Signal coherence across sources
  • Platform alignment
  • Entity disambiguation

AI readiness strongly influences whether a business is recognised or misinterpreted.

Pillar 3 — Continuous Optimisation

Whether the business maintains a stable, reliable, and evolving machine identity as models and contexts change.

  • Monitoring drift
  • Reinforcing corroboration
  • Maintaining digital integrity
  • Iterative alignment with evolving AI behaviours

AI systems evolve. Businesses need to evolve with them.

A Note on Completeness

The public structure of the EDI reflects only the external-facing elements of the discipline. Additional internal parameters support the stability, reliability, and consistency of the score across models and contexts.

These internal layers ensure that the EDI remains a dependable standard without introducing unnecessary complexity into the public framework.

This overview does not include the internal evaluation logic, which is maintained separately to preserve the integrity and consistency of the index.

What an EDI Score Reveals

An EDI score provides clarity on:

  • how well AI systems understand the business
  • how consistently the business is surfaced across models
  • where ambiguity or misinterpretation may occur
  • how resilient the machine identity is to model changes
  • whether competitors are more “machine-ready”
  • what structural improvements are required

It is a diagnostic tool for machine-era visibility.

The EDI as Part of a Formal Discipline

The EDI is a foundational component of EntityWorks’ work to formalise AI Discoverability as a discipline. It translates the discipline’s principles into a measurable standard that organisations can act on.

Within the broader discipline of AI Perception, the EDI provides a practical way to examine how AI systems form and express their understanding of a specific business entity in practice.

Together with the definition of AI Discoverability, the structured framework, the lexicon, and the methodology, the EDI helps establish the foundational model for how businesses prepare themselves for AI-first visibility.
