What the EDI Is

AI systems are rapidly becoming the primary way people discover, evaluate, and interact with businesses. Yet until now, there has been no standard for understanding how clearly a business is interpreted and surfaced by AI models.

The EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI) is a structured 0–100 assessment of how clearly and consistently AI systems can understand and surface a business entity.

It is not an SEO metric. It is not a marketing measure. It is a machine-comprehension standard designed for the AI era.

Why the EDI Exists

AI systems rely on structured data, machine-readable context, entity definitions, metadata integrity, cross-source corroboration, and consistent naming and signals.

But businesses have never had a formal way to measure:

  • how understandable they are to AI
  • how coherent their machine identity is
  • whether AI models can reliably surface them
  • where ambiguity, drift, or inconsistency is occurring
  • whether their digital structure supports accurate AI interpretation

The EDI establishes the structural foundation required for businesses to be represented accurately in AI-led environments.

The Three Structural Pillars of the EDI

These pillars form the core dimensions of AI Discoverability. They define how AI systems interpret, understand, and surface businesses with clarity and consistency. We publish these three as the external-facing structure of the discipline.

Pillar 1 — Structured Data

The clarity, depth, and accuracy of the machine-readable information a business provides.

  • Schema
  • Metadata
  • Structured definitions
  • Canonical identity fields
  • Machine-friendly descriptions

AI systems depend on structure to understand entities.

Pillar 2 — AI Readiness

How well a business’s digital presence aligns with the interpretive patterns AI models use today.

  • Naming consistency
  • Conceptual clarity
  • Signal coherence across sources
  • Platform alignment
  • Entity disambiguation

AI readiness strongly influences whether a business is recognised or misinterpreted.

Pillar 3 — Continuous Optimisation

Whether the business maintains a stable, reliable, and evolving machine identity as models and contexts change.

  • Monitoring drift
  • Reinforcing corroboration
  • Maintaining digital integrity
  • Iterative alignment with evolving AI behaviours

AI systems evolve. Businesses need to evolve with them.

A Note on Completeness

The EDI contains additional internal layers that ensure the score remains:

  • robust
  • consistent
  • model-agnostic
  • resistant to manipulation
  • reflective of how modern AI systems process complex entities

These parameters remain proprietary to ensure the integrity, stability, and long-term reliability of the EDI as a machine-era standard.

Nothing in this public overview reveals EntityWorks’ internal evaluation logic or weighting model.

What an EDI Score Reveals

An EDI score provides clarity on:

  • how well AI systems understand the business
  • how consistently the business is surfaced across models
  • where ambiguity or misinterpretation may occur
  • how resilient the machine identity is to model changes
  • whether competitors are more “machine-ready”
  • what structural improvements are required

It is a diagnostic tool for machine-era visibility.

Why the EDI Matters

As AI becomes the primary interface for discovery:

Businesses with unclear, inconsistent, or fragmented machine identities may:

  • be misinterpreted
  • be confused with competitors
  • lose contextual relevance
  • appear less frequently across AI interfaces
  • experience reduced trust or discoverability

Businesses with strong machine identities are more likely to:

  • be understood
  • be differentiated
  • be surfaced correctly
  • be consistently represented
  • maintain visibility as models evolve

The EDI enables businesses to strengthen these foundations proactively.

The EDI as Part of a New Discipline

Define, structure, and formalise the discipline of AI Discoverability.

Through the definition, the structured framework, the lexicon, the methodology, and the measurable standard, EntityWorks establishes the foundational model for how businesses prepare themselves for AI-first visibility.
