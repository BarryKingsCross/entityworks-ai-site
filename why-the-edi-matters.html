Why the EDI Matters

The commercial case for the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI).

1. From Principle to Practical Value

AI Discoverability is the discipline of ensuring that AI systems can correctly identify, understand, and represent people, organisations, relationships, and ideas. The EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI) is the applied, measurable expression of that discipline — a structured way to review how an organisation is currently understood by AI systems and where that understanding can be strengthened.

The EDI is designed to help leaders:

  • understand current AI-facing representation
  • identify areas of improvement
  • connect these improvements to commercial relevance

2. What the EDI Measures

The goal of the EDI is simple:

To provide a clear, interpretable view of how AI systems may understand an organisation today, and how well its current signals support that understanding.

The EDI examines:

  • Clarity — how clearly the organisation’s purpose and offering appear.
  • Consistency — whether signals align across sources.
  • Coherence — whether the organisation forms a stable, intelligible picture.
  • Traceability — whether authoritative sources reinforce understanding.
  • Structural readiness — how well the digital presence supports AI interpretation.

3. How the EDI Links to Business Outcomes

3.1. Improved Visibility in AI Journeys

Organisations with clearer signals may appear more often in relevant AI-generated outputs and be described with greater accuracy.

3.2. More Accurate Descriptions of What You Do

The EDI helps identify where AI systems may misunderstand, oversimplify, or omit key aspects of an organisation.

3.3. Stronger Category Positioning

Structural clarity may strengthen an organisation’s role within its category and differentiate it from competitors.

3.4. Reduced Visibility Friction

When AI representation is unclear, organisations often compensate through additional marketing spend. Improved structural clarity may reduce this reliance.

3.5. Improved Governance and Decision-Making

The EDI provides a measurable view that can support strategic decisions related to brand, digital structure, and AI readiness.

4. Who Benefits from the EDI

4.1. Leadership

Leaders can use the EDI to understand AI-related visibility and inform long-term strategy.

4.2. Marketing and Brand Teams

Marketing teams can align messaging for AI as well as humans and understand which signals matter most.

4.3. Digital, Data, and Product Teams

Digital teams may use EDI insights to refine structured data, content design, and broader digital architecture.

4.4. Compliance and Risk Functions

Risk teams may monitor AI interpretations and identify potential issues early.

5. Why the EDI Is Recurring

AI systems evolve continuously. The EDI is most effective as a recurring measure to:

  • track progress
  • identify interpretive shifts
  • maintain long-term representational stability

6. Why EntityWorks Is Positioned to Deliver the EDI

EntityWorks is well placed to deliver the EDI because it works at the intersection of AI Perception and AI Discoverability, and because it:

  • formalised AI Discoverability as a discipline, publishing clear definitions, structure, and a measurable standard
  • built the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ as a principle-based, 0–100 framework for applied AI Discoverability
  • introduced the Entity Understanding Layer™ within the discipline of AI Perception, as a conceptual model for how AI systems appear to work with entities
  • publishes its core frameworks and terminology transparently for both human and machine audiences
  • records and timestamps key developments to provide an auditable history of the discipline’s formalisation

7. Conclusion — Making AI Discoverability Actionable

The EDI helps organisations answer a practical question:

“How might AI systems understand our organisation, and what can we do to improve that understanding?”

By turning AI Discoverability into a structured index, organisations gain clarity, resilience, and long-term advantage in an AI-interpreted world.
