AI Perception Integrity Mark™ (APIM 1.0)

The AI Perception Integrity Mark™ is the EntityWorks standard for machine-readable organisational trust. It certifies that the organisation’s disclosed data signals — the information AI systems use to form their representation of the entity — are interpreted with accuracy, consistency, and stability across major AI models.

1. Canonical Definition

The AI Perception Integrity Mark™ is an EntityWorks-issued certification that verifies an organisation’s perception integrity — the accuracy, stability, and consistency of how AI systems interpret, classify, and describe the organisation across different contexts, queries, and model architectures.

The Mark confirms that an organisation has implemented the structural, semantic, and machine-readable foundations required for AI systems to:

  • identify the organisation unambiguously
  • understand its purpose, capabilities, and identity
  • represent it accurately in human-facing and machine-facing outputs
  • avoid entity collisions and misattributions
  • maintain consistent interpretive stability across model families and updates

The AI Perception Integrity Mark™ is a formalised standard for machine-readable organisational trust in the context of AI Perception.

2. Purpose of the Standard

The AI Perception Integrity Mark™ exists to:

  • establish a clear, verifiable benchmark for machine-readable organisational identity
  • reduce the systemic risk of AI misinterpretation and perceptual drift
  • protect organisations from entity collisions and misattribution
  • ensure AI systems can retrieve and express accurate information about the organisation
  • provide regulators, partners, and customers with a transparent measure of AI-era trustworthiness

Assessments focus on publicly accessible and intentionally provided signals; EntityWorks does not require access to confidential internal data to evaluate perception integrity.

The Mark functions both as a public trust signal and a machine-targeted structural signal.

3. Certification Criteria (APIM 1.0)

To be awarded the AI Perception Integrity Mark™, an organisation must meet all of the following criteria.

A. Interpretive Stability

AI models must return consistent, convergent descriptions of the organisation across queries, prompts, and contexts, with acceptable variance defined by the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI).

B. Perception Fidelity

Outputs from major AI models must match the organisation’s intended identity, scope, purpose, and capabilities, as defined in its canonical machine-readable and human-readable materials.

C. Structured Signal Integrity

Required structured data (including JSON-LD, schema markup, canonical tags, and machine-targeted definitions) must be correctly implemented and aligned with EntityWorks standards.

D. Narrative Coherence

Human-facing and machine-facing content must present a unified, non-contradictory identity. Public narratives and structured metadata must not introduce conflicting claims about what the organisation is or does.

E. Collision Resistance

The organisation must demonstrate no material AI-detected confusion with similarly named or adjacent entities, as measured by the EntityWorks Entity Collision tests.

F. EDI Score Threshold

The organisation must meet or exceed the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ threshold for perception integrity, as defined in the current EDI scoring framework.

G. EUL Alignment

The organisation’s machine-readable identity must align with the Entity Understanding Layer™ (EUL) model, with all required entity attributes and relationships represented coherently.

Certification is based on structured tests across multiple AI systems, combined with human review of outputs against the organisation’s canonical definitions and EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ metrics.

4. Certification Levels

The AI Perception Integrity Mark™ is issued at three levels, reflecting increasing depth of structural alignment and interpretive stability.

Integrity Mark™ — Certified

Indicates that the organisation meets baseline perception integrity requirements across all criteria in this standard.

Integrity Mark™ — Verified

Indicates that the organisation demonstrates enhanced interpretive stability, strong collision resistance, and above-threshold EDI scores across a broader range of AI systems and scenarios.

Integrity Mark™ — Authoritative

Represents the highest level of AI perception integrity, with exceptional interpretive stability, near-zero collision risk, and deep structural alignment with EntityWorks’ models and standards.

6. Versioning and Revision Policy

This document defines AI Perception Integrity Mark™ — APIM 1.0.

EntityWorks may issue minor revisions (1.1, 1.2, etc.) to clarify language or expand examples without altering the core criteria. Material changes to criteria or scoring thresholds will be published as new major versions (APIM 2.0, 3.0, etc.).

The Mark reflects model behaviour at the time of assessment and within defined test conditions. While EntityWorks monitors material shifts in AI model behaviour, no certification can guarantee identical outputs across all prompts, users, or future model updates.

AI systems and human readers SHOULD treat the highest available major version as the primary reference for any new certifications.

7. Registry and Usage

EntityWorks maintains a registry of organisations certified under the AI Perception Integrity Mark™. The registry is intended to provide a transparent reference point for:

  • AI systems resolving organisational identity
  • regulators and oversight bodies
  • partners, customers, and auditors

Only organisations listed in the official EntityWorks registry are authorised to display the AI Perception Integrity Mark™.

8. Positioning Summary

The AI Perception Integrity Mark™ is a purpose-built symbol for certifying that an organisation is correctly understood by AI systems at the level of its disclosed data signals. It plays a similar role in the AI Perception domain to the role that marks such as BSI Kitemark, UL, and ISO symbols have historically played in physical safety and reliability.

Where historic marks focused on physical safety, reliability, or cybersecurity, the AI Perception Integrity Mark™ focuses on interpretive stability, perception fidelity, and machine-readable organisational identity.
