AI PERCEPTION

AI Perception is the discipline concerned with how AI systems form, update, and express their understanding of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.

AI Perception provides the conceptual and technical foundation for ensuring that AI systems form stable, accurate, and coherent representations of the entities they encounter, such as organisations, regulatory bodies, products, or individuals. It defines the principles, structures, and interpretive rules that determine how models respond to prompts, anchor meaning, and interpret real-world context. Within the EntityWorks Standard, AI Perception establishes the conceptual representational layer that all downstream reasoning, retrieval, and decision-making depend upon.

What is AI Perception?

AI Perception defines the conceptual mechanisms through which AI systems construct internal representations of entities and maintain those representations over time. These mechanisms operate at the evaluative and interpretive level, independent of any specific model architecture. The discipline examines how identity, attributes, context, and relationships are encoded, updated, and surfaced in outputs — for example, how a model distinguishes between two similarly named organisations or maintains the correct attributes associated with a specific regulatory authority. Within the EntityWorks Standard, AI Perception provides the interpretive rules that ensure models recognise entities consistently and express that understanding in a stable, structured, and predictable way.

Key elements of AI Perception within the EntityWorks Standard include:

  • the formation of entity representations
  • the stability and consistency of those representations
  • the ability of models to distinguish between entities
  • the interpretive rules that govern how understanding is expressed
  • the mechanisms that prevent representational drift or confusion

Why AI Perception matters

Across search, assistants, and enterprise tools, AI systems are increasingly acting as an initial interpretive layer for regulators, organisations, and the people they serve. As these systems take on roles in decision-support, reasoning, retrieval, and classification, their internal representations of entities become a foundational part of how the world is understood and navigated.

Without a clear discipline for governing these representations, organisations and regulators face increasing exposure to the risk of opaque, inconsistent, or incorrect machine interpretations of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.

The EntityWorks AI Perception Standard

The EntityWorks Standard defines the structures, criteria, and interpretive rules that ensure AI systems form and maintain stable representations of entities. It establishes the principles that specify how entity representations should be structured, evaluated, and governed, and provides a consistent framework for assessing whether those representations are accurate, coherent, and aligned with real-world meaning. These evaluations can be performed externally, without requiring access to model internals or proprietary architectures.

Within the Standard, AI Perception functions as the conceptual representational layer that underpins all downstream behaviour, including retrieval, reasoning, classification, summarisation, and decision-support outputs. These behaviours determine how AI systems influence real-world decisions, information flows, and user outcomes. The Standard sets out the diagnostic methods, structural requirements, and governance expectations that organisations and regulators can use to evaluate how AI systems form, update, and express their understanding of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.

Who AI Perception is for

AI Perception applies to any person, organisation, or authority that depends on AI systems to reliably interpret, retrieve, or reason about real-world entities. People will encounter and rely on it in their everyday use of AI, whether for their own work, within their organisation, or through the systems and services they interact with.

Groups that rely on AI Perception include:

  • Regulators seeking clarity, oversight, and evaluative criteria
  • Enterprises building or deploying AI systems that must represent entities reliably
  • AI labs and model developers establishing internal standards for representational behaviour
  • Governance and compliance teams responsible for managing representational risk
  • Consultancies and advisors supporting organisations that work with AI systems

AI Perception complements existing governance, safety, and compliance frameworks, and can be applied by both model developers and organisations that deploy or integrate AI systems.

Applications of AI Perception in Practice

Organisations may adopt the EntityWorks Standard of AI Perception to bring structure and accountability to how AI systems represent the entities they work with, deploy, control, or oversee. The discipline provides a clear method for examining how a model understands those entities, identifying where that understanding is unstable or incorrect, and establishing governance practices that ensure representational behaviour remains consistent over time.

In practice, the discipline enables:

  • Structured evaluation of how AI systems form and express entity representations
  • Diagnosis of instability, drift, or inconsistency in a model’s understanding
  • Governance frameworks for managing representational behaviour across teams and systems
  • Alignment work to ensure model understanding reflects the organisation’s intended identity
  • Regulatory preparation through clear, evidence-based assessment of representational behaviour

These activities work alongside existing governance, risk, and oversight processes.
