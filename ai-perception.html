AI Perception

Human-Facing Edition (v1.1)

1. Definition

AI Perception is the discipline concerned with how AI systems form, update, and express their understanding of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas. Within the EntityWorks Standard, this discipline provides the conceptual structures and interpretive boundaries used to describe how models represent entities and surface that understanding in their outputs.

2. Role Within the EntityWorks Standard

AI Perception functions as the conceptual representational layer of the EntityWorks Standard. It sets out the terminology, structures, and interpretive logic used to describe how AI systems form internal representations of entities, maintain those representations over time, and express that understanding in responses, classifications, or other outputs.

3. Core Elements of the Discipline

  • formation of entity representations
  • stability and consistency of those representations
  • distinguishability between entities
  • interpretive rules governing expression of understanding
  • mechanisms that prevent representational drift

4. Why AI Perception Matters

As AI systems support search, retrieval, reasoning, and organisational workflows, their internal representations of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas influence how information is interpreted. In contexts where these systems contribute to analytical or decision-support work, misaligned or unstable representations may create inconsistencies in how entities are understood.
