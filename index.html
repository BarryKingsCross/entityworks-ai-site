AI Discoverability — The New Discipline

AI is becoming the de facto search interface. People ask for what they want; AI generates answers. Whether a business appears clearly in those answers increasingly depends on how well AI can understand it.

AI Discoverability is the discipline of ensuring that a business presents itself in a way that modern AI systems can interpret clearly and consistently.

Why This Matters Now

AI systems now act as intermediaries between customers and information. They interpret data, summarise it, and return answers based on their understanding of an entity.

If AI cannot interpret a business clearly, that business becomes less consistently visible across AI-generated results. The EDI provides a structured way to understand and respond to that shift.

What the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ Is

The EDI is a structured assessment that measures how clearly and reliably a business can be understood by modern AI systems. It turns an emerging, hard-to-see problem into something measurable, structured, and actionable.

The EDI provides a practical starting point for improving how a business appears in AI-driven environments.

What the EDI Measures — The EDI Pillars

The Index evaluates three structural factors that influence how AI understands an entity:

  • Structured Data — Foundational information organised and made machine-readable.
  • AI Readiness — Clear and consistent representation across key sources.
  • Continuous Optimisation — Maintaining clarity as AI systems evolve.

How the EDI Assessment Works

  1. We analyse publicly available information from key sources.
  2. We apply the three EDI Pillars.
  3. We produce a Discoverability Score (0–100).
  4. We generate a Profile Summary.
  5. We provide a Priority Path.
  6. We deliver structured recommendations.

Improvements Businesses Can Consider

  • Clarifying descriptions
  • Improving consistency across sources
  • Strengthening structured data
  • Correcting contradictions and outdated information
  • Reinforcing category placement
  • Addressing representation drift

What the EDI Does Not Measure

The EDI does not evaluate product quality, brand strength, marketing performance, financial health, or customer sentiment.

It focuses solely on how well AI systems can understand and surface the business.

Claims Discipline

EntityWorks maintains strict standards for the EDI. All statements are verifiable, truthful, proportionate, non-absolute, and non-misleading.
