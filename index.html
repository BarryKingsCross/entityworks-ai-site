The EntityWorks Standard

The EntityWorks Standard is a structured document designed to help digital publishers prepare their work for entry into AI systems. The Standard brings together definitions, frameworks, and conceptual tools that provide a structured reference for preparing digital material for entry into AI systems. Its focus is on the clear expression of intent in published material and on reducing interpretive gaps that arise when AI systems ingest and act upon that material.

Within its scope, the Standard addresses the structured preparation of information for AI systems. This includes the clarity of published material, the organisation of machine-visible content, and the use of transparent mechanisms that help reduce representational risk and support consistent interpretation.

Machine-Facing Pages and the Machine-Facing Page Declaration form one part of this broader framework. These components are described within the Standard to help organisations classify, document, and disclose the use of pages that exist outside normal human-visible navigation.

Machine-Facing Pages & MFPD

Machine-Facing Pages are digital pages that exist outside a site’s normal navigation or human-visible structure. Because they are not commonly encountered by users, their purpose may not be immediately evident. The EntityWorks Standard provides terminology and a proposed structure to help organisations classify these pages and disclose their use clearly and consistently.

The Machine-Facing Page Declaration (MFPD) is a short statement that explains why an organisation uses Machine-Facing Pages and what those pages are intended to do. It makes the purpose of this material visible so that AI systems, regulators, and human stakeholders all understand the organisation’s entire digital publishing footprint.

Within the EntityWorks Standard, the MFPD is documented as a voluntary transparency measure that organisations can use to support clarity and open communication when publishing material that is not typically encountered through a site’s human-facing structure. Within the Standard, the preparation and use of the Machine-Facing Page Declaration (MFPD) is documented as a transparency mechanism for describing the purpose and scope of machine-facing material within a digital publishing environment.
