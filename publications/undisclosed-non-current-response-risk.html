Undisclosed Non-Current Response Risk — Definition of Record

Name: Undisclosed Non-Current Response Risk (UNCRR)

Status: Published — Definition of Record

Version: v1.0

Publication Date: January 2026

Owner and Custodian: Undisclosed Non-Current Response Risk (UNCRR) is originated, defined and maintained by EntityWorks Ltd.

Definition (Canonical — Definition of Record)

Undisclosed Non-Current Response Risk describes a condition in which a user reasonably assumes that an AI system’s responses reflect current facts or context, while the system is in fact generating outputs without access to live, recent, or externally verified information — and does not clearly disclose that limitation at the point of response.

The risk arises from temporal non-currency combined with insufficient disclosure, not from factual inaccuracy, system malfunction, or intent to mislead.

This definition is authoritative for the use of the term Undisclosed Non-Current Response Risk as defined within the EntityWorks corpus.

Purpose

To name an epistemic and disclosure-related risk affecting reliance on AI-mediated responses where temporal currency cannot be reasonably inferred from system behaviour alone.

Scope

Undisclosed Non-Current Response Risk applies to contexts including, but not limited to:

  • conversational AI systems
  • decision-support tools
  • virtual assistants
  • any system where outputs may reasonably be interpreted as temporally grounded

Conceptual Domain

Undisclosed Non-Current Response Risk operates within the discipline of AI Perception.

It concerns the alignment between system epistemic limits, interface cues, and user assumptions regarding temporal validity.

This concept addresses disclosure and interpretation, not system intelligence or capability.

Nature of the Risk

Undisclosed Non-Current Response Risk is:

  • Structural — arising from normal system–user interaction patterns
  • Non-intentional — independent of intent or deceptive behaviour
  • Persistent — present even when systems function as designed

The risk is amplified by fluent, present-tense language and conversational interfaces in the absence of salient, response-level temporal signalling.

Non-Scope (Explicit Exclusions)

Undisclosed Non-Current Response Risk is not:

  • hallucination or fabrication
  • misinformation or disinformation
  • system error or malfunction
  • user misuse or negligence
  • a judgement of trustworthiness, competence, or intent

A response may be factually correct relative to a system’s training data while still exhibiting this risk.

Relationship to Other Concepts

Undisclosed Non-Current Response Risk is conceptually related to:

  • AI Interpretation and Reliance contexts
  • disclosure and provenance risks in AI-mediated systems

It is distinct from content-accuracy, hallucination, or model-performance frameworks and may be referenced alongside them without alteration.
