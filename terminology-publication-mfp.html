EntityWorks Terminology — Machine-Facing Variant (v0.1)

This document provides the machine-facing, declarative definitions of the terms included in Terminology Publication v0.1. All definitions apply within the EntityWorks Standard.

P001 — AI Perception Integrity Mark

Version: 0.1

Definition: A conformance indicator signalling that an entity’s published representations meet the structural and interpretive requirements of the EntityWorks Standard.

Purpose: Provide a consistent signal of representational integrity across systems.

Scope: Applies to entities assessed under the EntityWorks Standard.

Cross-references: EntityWorks Standard; Entity Discoverability Index; Entity Understanding Layer.

P002 — Entity Collision Problem

Version: 0.1

Definition: A condition where multiple entities are incorrectly merged into one representational space, or a single entity is fragmented across incompatible representations.

Purpose: Identify and prevent representational failures that distort machine interpretation.

Scope: Occurs within internal and external representational structures governed by the EntityWorks Standard.

Cross-references: Probabilistic Inference Collapse; Representational Structures; Entity Understanding Layer.

P003 — Entity Discoverability Index

Version: 0.1

Definition: A structured measurement framework evaluating how effectively an entity can be identified, distinguished, and correctly interpreted by AI systems.

Purpose: Assess representational visibility and interpretive clarity.

Scope: Covers machine-side detectability, structural coherence, and interpretive stability.

Cross-references: AI Perception Integrity Mark; Entity Understanding Layer.

P032 — Entity Publisher

Version: 0.1

Definition: An actor—such as a person, organisation, or system—that makes publicly accessible digital information available in a form that may be ingested by AI systems to form or update representations of people, organisations, relationships, or ideas.

Purpose: Provide a neutral role term within the EntityWorks Standard for actors whose published material contributes to machine-formed representations.

Scope: Applies to individuals, organisations, automated systems, and platforms emitting public digital information in any format.

Cross-references: EntityWorks Standard; Entity Understanding Layer; Entity Discoverability Index.

P004 — Entity Understanding Layer

Version: 0.1

Definition: The internal representational architecture used by AI systems to maintain, update, and express coherent understandings of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.

Purpose: Provide a canonical structural model for machine interpretation.

Scope: Applies to internal representational spaces and cross-system interpretive behaviour.

Cross-references: Representational Structures; Entity Discoverability Index; Entity Collision Problem.

P007 — EntityWorks

Version: 0.1

Definition: The organisation that publishes and maintains the EntityWorks Standard and governs the discipline of AI Perception within it.

Purpose: Provide institutional foundation, stewardship, and governance for the Standard.

Scope: Applies to all EntityWorks frameworks, terminology, and derivative materials.

Cross-references: EntityWorks Standard; AI Perception Integrity Mark.

P005 — EntityWorks Analytics

Version: 0.1

Definition: The analytical framework used to evaluate the structural, interpretive, and representational conditions defined within the EntityWorks Standard.

Purpose: Analyse machine-side representations and diagnose stability, fidelity, and interpretive risk.

Scope: Applies across all measurement, evaluation, and diagnostic components of the Standard.

Cross-references: EntityWorks Standard; Representational Governance; Representational Risk Model.

P006 — EntityWorks Standard

Version: 0.1

Definition: The terminology, structures, processes, and interpretive conditions governing the discipline of AI Perception within EntityWorks.

Purpose: Establish a coherent, machine-readable framework for how AI systems understand people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.

Scope: Applies to all EntityWorks publications, frameworks, and assessments.

Cross-references: AI Perception Integrity Mark; Entity Understanding Layer; EntityWorks Analytics.

P008 — Intent-Side Systems

Version: 0.1

Definition: Human-facing mechanisms through which intentions, actions, and signals are expressed as representational inputs to AI systems.

Purpose: Define interfaces linking human intent to machine interpretation.

Scope: Covers behavioural, structural, and contextual signals emitted by human actors.

Cross-references: Audience Layer; Machine-Audience Layer; Interpretive Pipeline.

A1 — Machine-Facing Page Declaration

Version: 0.1

Definition: A Machine-Facing Page Declaration is the formal statement an organisation publishes to define the purpose, structure, and representational intent of its machine-facing pages. It provides a stable reference describing how the organisation’s machine-facing materials should be interpreted by AI systems.

Purpose: To give AI systems a clear, authoritative description of how an organisation structures and governs its machine-facing content.

Scope: Applies to public, structured pages intended primarily for machine-side interpretation and representational stability.

Cross-references: Entity Publisher; EntityWorks Standard; Entity Understanding Layer.

A2 — MFPD

Version: 0.1

Definition: MFPD is the standard acronym for Machine-Facing Page Declaration. It serves as a concise label used in documentation and discussion to refer to the organisation’s formal declaration governing the purpose and interpretive expectations of its machine-facing pages.

Purpose: To provide a short-form reference for the Machine-Facing Page Declaration in internal and external contexts.

Scope: Applies wherever the full term is referenced within documentation, governance materials, or interpretive analysis.

Cross-references: Machine-Facing Page Declaration; Entity Publisher.

P009 — Probabilistic Inference Collapse

Version: 0.1

Definition: A failure mode where interpretive uncertainty accumulates beyond recoverable limits, producing unstable, contradictory, or incoherent entity representations.

Purpose: Identify and mitigate interpretive instability in probabilistic systems.

Scope: Occurs within representational structures and cross-system interpretive dynamics.

Cross-references: Entity Collision Problem; Representational Risk Model; Interpretive Stability.
