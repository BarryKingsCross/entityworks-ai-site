About EntityWorks

EntityWorks exists because AI systems are now being relied upon by people and organisations who did not build them.

As soon as that happens, informal explanations stop being sufficient.

Shared reliance requires shared interpretive structure.

When decisions, trust, coordination, or legitimacy depend on what an AI system understands, there must be a shared interpretive reference that remains stable across systems, contexts, and time.

EntityWorks was created to provide that reference.

The Problem EntityWorks Addresses

AI systems increasingly act as interpretive intermediaries — shaping how people, organisations, relationships, and ideas are represented, surfaced, prioritised, or acted upon.

However:

  • those interpretations are rarely documented in a shared way,
  • meaning drifts across systems and updates,
  • and reliance emerges before interpretive structure does.

This creates a structural risk.

People and organisations begin to depend on AI-mediated interpretations that have no stable, external reference. Over time, this makes responsibility, comparison, and accountability impossible.

EntityWorks exists to resolve that risk by defining interpretive infrastructure where none previously existed.

What EntityWorks Is

EntityWorks is a standards-level interpretive infrastructure organisation.

It defines, publishes, and stewards formal frameworks that allow AI-mediated representations to be:

  • identified,
  • categorised,
  • referenced,
  • and relied upon coherently.

EntityWorks does not operate at the level of execution or optimisation.

It operates at the level of interpretive structure.

How EntityWorks Operates

EntityWorks operates by:

  • originating interpretive frameworks and shared reference models,
  • maintaining canonical definitions and criteria,
  • issuing formal interpretive artefacts grounded in those frameworks,
  • licensing the use of EntityWorks standards, instruments, and marks,
  • and stewarding framework evolution over time.

These activities provide orientation, not instruction.

They allow organisations, institutions, regulators, and system owners to act independently within a shared interpretive space. EntityWorks does not participate in those actions; it maintains the reference they act against.

Why This Matters

Every major technical shift eventually requires interpretive infrastructure.

Markets require shared classifications before pricing stabilises.

Engineering requires standards before systems interoperate.

Governance requires reference frameworks before responsibility can be assigned.

AI is now at that point.

EntityWorks exists to define the interpretive layer that makes long-term reliance possible.

The Role EntityWorks Plays

EntityWorks defines how AI-mediated interpretations are understood once others have to rely on them.

It does not tell organisations what to do.

It ensures that when they act, they are doing so within a coherent interpretive structure rather than ambiguity.

EntityWorks publications, standards, instruments, and artefacts provide interpretive reference only. They do not constitute advice, certification, approval, compliance determination, or instruction.
