EntityWorks Lexicon

A reference glossary for the core terminology used in the discipline of AI Discoverability.

These definitions support the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI), the Entity Understanding Layer™, and the Presence Stack™ model.

Core Terms

AI Discoverability
The discipline concerned with how clearly and consistently AI systems can understand, interpret, and surface a business or entity.
Machine-Legibility
The degree to which a business’s information is structured, unambiguous, and readable by AI systems, independent of how it appears to humans.
Entity Clarity
How confidently an AI system can determine what an organisation is, what it does, and what it offers based on available information.
Entity Understanding Layer™
EntityWorks’ interpretive model for how AI systems appear to build and update their internal understanding of entities. Instead of exposing proprietary internals, it organises observable behaviour into six higher-level patterns we can reasonably infer are happening: Identification, Attribution, Contextualisation, Weighting, Updating, and Propagation. Together, these behaviours offer a structured way to think about how AI systems internally process entities and allow leaders to reason about “how AI sees us” without overstating claims about any specific model. See EUL overview.
Representation Drift
The gradual divergence between how a business describes itself and how AI systems come to describe it over time.
Claims Discipline
The practice of ensuring that all statements about AI Discoverability, the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI), and related outcomes remain verifiable, proportionate, and non-misleading.
Structured Data
Information expressed in machine-readable formats (such as schema.org JSON-LD, metadata, or well-formed tables) that helps AI systems reliably interpret entities.
AI Readiness
The extent to which a business’s public footprint is clear, up to date, and aligned across the key sources AI systems rely on.
Continuous Optimisation
The ongoing process of monitoring and adjusting a business’s machine-legible presence as AI systems, models, and information sources evolve.
Discoverability Score (0–100)
A composite score indicating how clearly AI systems can understand and surface a business or entity, as measured by the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™.
EDI Pillars
The three structural dimensions used by the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™: (1) Structured Data, (2) AI Readiness, and (3) Continuous Optimisation.
Profile Summary
A concise narrative describing how AI systems currently perceive and present a business, based on EDI findings.
Priority Path
A sequenced set of recommendations designed to improve a business’s Discoverability Score in the most efficient and impactful order.
Presence Stack™
A layered model describing the components that shape how AI systems see, interpret, and prioritise a business’s presence across the digital environment.
Inter-Source Alignment
The level of consistency between multiple public sources (such as websites, profiles, directories, and knowledge graphs) when they describe the same organisation.
Semantic Integrity
The preservation of meaning across different descriptions, channels, and formats, so that AI systems receive a coherent picture of the business.
Entity Surface Area
The total volume and diversity of information about a business that is available for AI systems to ingest, including both structured and unstructured data.
Category Creation
The act of defining, naming, and formalising a discipline or field where no coherent, shared structure previously existed.
Entity-Coherence Score
An internal metric concept for assessing how uniformly and consistently a business is represented across AI-indexed sources.
EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI)
A structured, measurable framework for assessing AI Discoverability and generating a Discoverability Score, Profile Summary, and Priority Path for a business. The EDI sits on top of the patterns described in the Entity Understanding Layer™, translating them into a practical 0–100 assessment. See EDI overview.
Legacy SEO
A shorthand for traditional, search-engine-focused optimisation practices that prioritise human search results and rankings rather than AI interpretation and machine-legibility.
