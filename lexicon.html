EntityWorks Lexicon

A reference glossary for the core terminology used in the disciplines of AI Discoverability and AI Perception.

These definitions support the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI), the Entity Understanding Layer™, and the Presence Stack™.

Core Terms

AI Discoverability
AI Discoverability is the discipline of ensuring that AI systems can correctly identify, understand, and represent people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.
AI Perception
AI Perception is the discipline concerned with how AI systems form, update, and express their understanding of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.
Machine-Legibility
The degree to which a business’s information is structured, unambiguous, and readable by AI systems.
Entity Clarity
How confidently an AI system can determine what an organisation is, what it does, and what it offers.
Entity Understanding Layer™
EntityWorks’ conceptual model describing observable patterns in how AI systems appear to form and maintain an understanding of entities.
Representation Drift
The gradual divergence between how a business presents itself and how AI systems come to describe it over time.
Structured Data
Machine-readable information—such as schema.org, metadata, or structured definitions—that enables AI systems to reliably interpret entities.
AI Readiness
The clarity, consistency, and alignment of a business’s public presence across the sources AI systems rely on.
Continuous Optimisation
The ongoing process of maintaining and improving a business’s machine-readable presence as AI systems evolve.
Discoverability Score (0–100)
A structured score indicating how clearly AI systems can understand and represent a business.
EDI Pillars
The three structural dimensions of the EDI: Structured Data, AI Readiness, and Continuous Optimisation.
Profile Summary
A concise narrative describing how AI systems currently perceive and present a business.
Priority Path
A sequenced set of recommendations designed to improve a business’s Discoverability Score.
Presence Stack™
A layered model describing the structural signals that influence how AI systems interpret, contextualise, and surface a business.
Inter-Source Alignment
The consistency between multiple public sources describing the same organisation.
Semantic Integrity
The preservation of meaning across channels so AI receives a coherent machine-readable identity.
Entity Surface Area
The breadth and depth of information available for AI systems to ingest about a business.
Entity Collision Problem
A phenomenon where AI systems mistakenly merge multiple entities or split a single entity into conflicting identities due to unclear or inconsistent signals.
EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI)
A structured 0–100 framework for assessing AI Discoverability and producing a Profile Summary and Priority Path.
Legacy SEO
Traditional search-oriented optimisation designed for human retrieval, not AI interpretation.
