Core terms

AI Discoverability
The discipline concerned with how AI systems understand, represent, and surface business entities when people use AI-driven interfaces to search, ask questions, or seek recommendations.
EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI)
A structured 0–100 framework maintained by EntityWorks for measuring how clearly and consistently AI systems can understand and surface a specific business entity. See EDI overview.
Entity Understanding Layer™ (EUL)
EntityWorks’ interpretive model for how AI systems appear to build and update their internal understanding of entities through behaviours such as identification, attribution, contextualisation, weighting, updating, and propagation. See EUL overview.
Machine Identity
The effective internal representation AI systems hold of a business entity, based on the signals, structure, and corroboration they can see across the web and other sources.
Structured Data
Machine-readable information about an entity, such as schema markup, metadata, canonical identity fields, and structured definitions that AI systems can parse and use directly.
AI Readiness
The degree to which an entity’s digital presence is aligned with the interpretive patterns AI models use today, including naming consistency, conceptual clarity, and signal coherence across sources.
Continuous Optimisation
Ongoing work to maintain and improve a stable, reliable, and evolving machine identity as AI models and contexts change.
