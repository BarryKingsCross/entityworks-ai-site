EntityWorks Lexicon

A reference glossary for the core terminology used in the disciplines of AI Discoverability and AI Perception.

These definitions support the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI), the Entity Understanding Layer™, and the Presence Stack™.

Core Terms

AI Discoverability
AI Discoverability is the discipline of ensuring that AI systems can correctly identify, understand, and represent people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.
AI Perception
AI Perception is the discipline concerned with how AI systems form, update, and express their understanding of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.
Machine-Legibility
The degree to which a business’s information is structured, unambiguous, and readable by AI systems, independent of how it appears to humans.
Entity Clarity
How confidently an AI system can determine what an organisation is, what it does, and what it offers, based on the information available to it.
Entity Understanding Layer™ (EUL)
A conceptual model within the discipline of AI Perception that provides a structured way to describe the behaviours AI systems rely on when forming, updating, or expressing their understanding of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas. It is organised around six core behaviours: Meaning Construction, Identity Embedding, Entity Corroboration, Semantic Stabilisation, Conversational Surfacing, and Trust Resolution.
Representation Drift
The gradual divergence between how a business describes itself and how AI systems come to describe it over time, as new information appears or older signals persist in the system.
Structured Data
Information expressed in machine-readable formats—such as schema.org JSON-LD, metadata fields, or well-formed tables— that helps AI systems reliably interpret entities and their attributes.
AI Readiness
The extent to which a business’s public footprint is clear, current, and aligned across the key sources AI systems rely on when forming their understanding of that business.
Continuous Optimisation
The ongoing process of monitoring and adjusting a business’s machine-legible presence as AI systems, models, and information ecosystems evolve, so that its representation remains accurate and resilient over time.
Discoverability Score (0–100)
A composite score, produced by the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™, indicating how clearly AI systems can understand and represent a business or entity across the core dimensions of AI Discoverability.
EDI Pillars
The three structural dimensions used by the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI): (1) Structured Data, (2) AI Readiness, and (3) Continuous Optimisation. Together, they capture the core conditions required for strong AI Discoverability.
Profile Summary
A concise narrative, derived from EDI findings, describing how AI systems currently perceive and present a business— highlighting what is clear, what is missing, and where the representation is drifting or fragmented.
Priority Path
A sequenced set of recommendations designed to improve a business’s Discoverability Score in the most efficient and impactful order, based on the EDI assessment.
Presence Stack™
A layered model describing the structural components that shape how AI systems see, interpret, and prioritise a business’s presence across the digital environment, with an emphasis on clarity, consistency, and contextual integrity.
Inter-Source Alignment
The level of consistency between multiple public sources (such as the primary website, profiles, directories, and knowledge graphs) when they describe the same organisation.
Semantic Integrity
The preservation of meaning across different descriptions, channels, and formats so that AI systems receive a coherent, stable picture of the business rather than fragmented or contradictory narratives.
Entity Surface Area
The total volume, diversity, and distribution of information about a business that is available for AI systems to ingest, including both structured and unstructured data across multiple sources.
Entity Collision Problem
A machine-era failure mode where AI systems mistakenly merge two or more distinct entities into one, or split a single entity into multiple conflicting identities. Collisions typically occur when machine-legible signals are unclear, inconsistent, or overlapping, and they highlight the need for strong AI Discoverability and coherent machine identity.
EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI)
A structured, measurable framework for assessing AI Discoverability and generating a Discoverability Score, Profile Summary, and Priority Path for a business. The EDI translates the principles of AI Discoverability into a practical 0–100 assessment of how clearly AI systems understand and represent a specific entity. See EDI overview.
Legacy SEO
A shorthand for traditional, search-engine-focused optimisation practices that primarily aim at human search rankings and keyword performance, rather than helping AI systems correctly interpret and represent entities.
