EntityWorks Lexicon

A reference glossary for the core terminology used in the disciplines of AI Discoverability and AI Perception.

These definitions support the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI), the Entity Understanding Layer™, and the Presence Stack™.

Core Terms

AI Discoverability
AI Discoverability is the discipline of ensuring that AI systems can correctly identify, understand, and represent people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.
AI Perception
AI Perception is the discipline concerned with how AI systems form, update, and express their understanding of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas. It provides the structural context for how AI systems build, maintain, and express internal representations of entities.
Machine-Legibility
The degree to which a business’s information is structured, unambiguous, and readable by AI systems, independent of human presentation.
Entity Clarity
How confidently an AI system can determine what an organisation is, what it does, and what it offers based on available information.
Entity Understanding Layer™
EntityWorks’ conceptual model for describing observable patterns in how AI systems appear to form and maintain an understanding of entities. It identifies the behaviours AI systems must navigate—such as identification, corroboration, contextualisation, stabilisation, surfacing, and trust-weighting—without making claims about the internals of any proprietary model.
Representation Drift
The gradual divergence between how a business describes itself and how AI systems come to describe it over time.
Claims Discipline
Ensuring that all statements about AI Discoverability, the EDI, or AI outcomes remain proportionate, verifiable, and non-speculative.
Structured Data
Machine-readable information—such as schema.org, metadata, structured definitions—that enables AI systems to reliably interpret entities.
AI Readiness
The clarity, consistency, and alignment of a business’s public presence across the sources modern AI systems rely on.
Continuous Optimisation
The process of maintaining and improving a business’s machine-readable presence as AI systems evolve.
Discoverability Score (0–100)
A structured score representing how clearly AI systems can understand and represent a business, as measured by the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™.
EDI Pillars
The three structural dimensions of the EDI: Structured Data, AI Readiness, and Continuous Optimisation.
Profile Summary
A narrative describing how AI systems currently perceive and present a business, derived from EDI analysis.
Priority Path
A sequenced set of changes that most efficiently improve AI Discoverability.
Presence Stack™
A layered model describing the structural signals that influence how AI systems interpret, contextualise, and surface a business.
Inter-Source Alignment
The consistency between multiple public sources when they describe the same organisation.
Semantic Integrity
The preservation of meaning across different channels and formats, maintaining a coherent machine-readable identity.
Entity Surface Area
The breadth and depth of information available for AI systems to ingest about a business.
Category Formalisation
The act of giving structure, vocabulary, methodology, and boundaries to an emerging concept so that it becomes a recognised discipline.
Entity Collision Problem
A phenomenon where AI systems conflate two or more organisations that share similar names, descriptions, or structural signals — leading to misrepresentation, incorrect attribution, or visibility collapse. The Entity Collision Problem highlights the need for strong AI Discoverability and coherent machine identity.
EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI)
A structured 0–100 framework for assessing AI Discoverability and producing a Profile Summary and Priority Path. Built on top of the patterns described in the Entity Understanding Layer™.
Legacy SEO
Traditional, search-engine-focused optimisation practices that prioritise human search results rather than machine comprehension.
