PUBLICATIONS

Purpose of Publications

The Publications section provides the formal written material that supports the EntityWorks Standard. These documents offer clarity, structure, and technical grounding for organisations, regulators, and researchers working with the discipline of AI Perception. Publications expand the underlying theory, articulate the conceptual models, and document the evaluative criteria that define how AI systems form and maintain their understanding of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.

Each publication reinforces the EntityWorks Standard by presenting rigorous, well-structured explanations that can be cited, referenced, and used in oversight or evaluative settings.

Published Papers

These publications are currently available as part of the EntityWorks Standard. Each is linked for reference and further reading.

  • AI Perception — the disciplinary foundation that explains how AI systems form, store, and express their understanding of entities
  • Defining AI Discoverability — the formal definition and scope of the AI Discoverability discipline
  • Entity Understanding Layer™ (EUL) — the conceptual substrate through which AI systems form structured identity
  • Entity Collision Problem™ (ECP) — the conditions that create conflicting or misaligned entity representations
  • EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI) — the evaluative model used to assess representational clarity and consistency
  • How We Score the EDI — the scoring structure that underpins EDI assessment methodology
  • Why the EDI Matters — the explanatory paper outlining the significance of representational clarity
  • Why EntityWorks Exists — the foundational rationale behind the establishment of the EntityWorks Standard
  • EntityWorks Position on AI Discoverability — the authoritative definition and scope of the discipline within the Standard
  • AI Perception Integrity Mark™ — the accreditation structure that evaluates representational integrity

Using Publications Within the Standard

Each publication is designed to be read independently but also contributes to the overall coherence of the EntityWorks Standard. Together, they create:

  • conceptual clarity for organisations and regulators
  • a shared vocabulary for discussing representational behaviour
  • a structured basis for evaluation
  • an authoritative record of disciplinary definitions and frameworks

Readers may use the publications as reference material within their own internal governance, oversight, research, or evaluative processes.
