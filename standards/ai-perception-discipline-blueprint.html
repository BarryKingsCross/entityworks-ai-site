The AI Perception Discipline Blueprint

EntityWorks Standard v1.0 — Published 2025-11-28

1. Purpose of This Blueprint

AI Perception, as defined within the EntityWorks Standard, is a fully structured technical discipline grounded in canonical definitions, formal ontology, and institutionally adoptable methodology.

This blueprint captures the complete structural architecture of the discipline, establishing the conceptual, ontological, and methodological foundation upon which all future work will proceed. It defines the stable reference from which academic, regulatory, and organisational extensions will be developed.

The blueprint sets out the discipline’s governing architecture through the EntityWorks:

  • canonical definitions establishing its scope and conceptual boundaries,
  • structural and representational primitives forming its ontological foundation, and
  • diagnostic, standards, and accreditation frameworks supporting consistent academic, regulatory, and organisational application.

Taken together, these elements constitute the completed foundational layer of the discipline. All subsequent research, policy development, and organisational adoption proceed from this reference architecture.

2. Scope and Components of the Discipline

2.1 Conceptual Foundations

This domain includes:

  • the EntityWorks canonical definitions of AI Perception and its subdomains,
  • the formal conceptual boundaries governing the discipline, and
  • the structured relationship between human-facing identity and machine-interpreted identity.

These foundations are already published within the EntityWorks Standard and form the definitional basis of the discipline.

2.2 Ontological Structure

This domain defines the representational substrate of AI Perception through:

  • the Entity Understanding Layer (EUL),
  • structural and representational primitives, and
  • rules governing entity classes, hierarchies, and interpretive constraints.

These ontological components represent the completed conceptual map of the discipline’s representational layer.

2.3 Diagnostic and Standards Frameworks

This domain provides:

  • analytic methods for inspecting machine-side representations,
  • evaluative frameworks for stability, drift, and consistency, and
  • accreditation criteria used within the EntityWorks Standard.

These frameworks establish the methods through which the discipline is assessed, compared, and governed.

3. Characteristics of a Discipline Standard

Within the EntityWorks Standard, AI Perception is defined as a discipline supported by:

  • a coherent ontology and representational structure,
  • consistent diagnostic and methodological logic, and
  • a formal publication sequence through which the discipline is extended.

The combination of these elements confirms the discipline’s structure as fully established and institutionally ready.

4. Intended Uses and Audiences

The blueprint is designed for structured application across institutional domains.

4.1 Regulators and Policy Bodies

To support the development of guidance and requirements relating to AI interpretability, representational integrity, and machine-readable identity governance.

4.2 Enterprises and Public Institutions

To align organisational identity with machine-side interpretation and ensure consistent representational behaviour across AI-mediated environments.

4.3 Researchers and Academics

To provide a defined, extensible reference architecture for comparative study, theoretical development, and discipline-level research.

4.4 Developers and Technical Teams

To align system behaviour with stable reference structures and ensure consistent interpretive outcomes.

4.5 Professional and Advisory Services

To ground assessment and advisory work in a coherent and standardised discipline.

5. Status of the EntityWorks Standard

EntityWorks publishes and maintains the EntityWorks Standard, which defines the discipline through:

  • a formal ontology,
  • diagnostic and evaluative frameworks,
  • and structural and methodological criteria.

The standard is openly published, institutionally oriented, and versioned to support stable long-term use. It establishes the authoritative structure from which future academic, regulatory, and organisational engagement will proceed. Institutions may adopt the standard where it provides value and clarity.

6. Development Pathway

The conceptual, ontological, and methodological foundations of the discipline have been fully established within the EntityWorks Standard.

Future publications extend this architecture through:

  • expanded ontology documentation,
  • methodological frameworks,
  • technical specifications, and
  • representational integrity guidance.

These publications formalise extensions of an already complete conceptual map, ensuring the discipline develops in a coherent, predictable, and structurally stable manner.
