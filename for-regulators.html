FOR REGULATORS

AI systems increasingly act as an initial interpretive layer across public services, regulated sectors, and organisational decision-making. As these systems take on roles in retrieval, reasoning, classification, and decision-support, regulators require clarity on how models form and maintain their understanding of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.

AI Perception provides this clarity by defining the structures and evaluative principles that determine how models represent entities and how that representation influences downstream behaviour. For regulators, this discipline offers a coherent way to assess whether AI systems interpret entities consistently, transparently, and in alignment with real-world meaning.

Regulatory challenges addressed by the EntityWorks Standard

Regulators face increasing pressure to evaluate the behaviour of AI systems without access to model internals or proprietary architectures. The EntityWorks Standard provides a structured way to assess representational behaviour using external, observable criteria.

Key challenges addressed by the Standard include:

  • interpretive opacity, where regulators cannot see how a model is understanding or representing an entity
  • inconsistent or unstable representations, which undermine reliability in regulated environments
  • cross-model divergence, where different AI systems form conflicting understandings of the same entity
  • lack of evaluative criteria, making it difficult to assess representational behaviour objectively
  • representational drift, where model understanding shifts over time without transparency or accountability

How regulators can use the Standard

Regulators can use the EntityWorks Standard to establish a structured basis for evaluating how AI systems represent entities within their oversight remit. The Standard provides a clear set of definitions, interpretive rules, and diagnostic criteria that allow regulators to assess representational behaviour externally, without requiring technical access to model internals or proprietary systems.

The Standard enables regulators to:

  • assess representational behaviour using consistent, non-technical criteria
  • set expectations and guidance for representational stability and accuracy
  • identify risks arising from inconsistent, opaque, or incorrect model understanding
  • support oversight and assurance work across sectors and jurisdictions
  • harmonise evaluative approaches around a shared conceptual framework

Regulators may draw on the Standard where it provides clarity, structure, or evaluative support within their existing oversight frameworks.

What the Standard provides to regulators

The EntityWorks Standard offers regulators a coherent framework for understanding, evaluating, and governing the representational behaviour of AI systems. It provides the conceptual structures and criteria needed to assess how models form, update, and express their understanding of people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.

Regulators can draw on:

  • canonical definitions that establish consistent terminology
  • structural frameworks describing how machine-interpreted identity is formed
  • diagnostic logic for assessing representational stability and behaviour
  • accreditation criteria that support transparent evaluation and assurance
  • forthcoming machine-readable specifications designed for technical reference and oversight
