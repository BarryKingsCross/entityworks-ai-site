Acknowledging Existing Work

AI Discoverability has been a topic of discussion for a variety of organisations, with some offering assessments, audits, and frameworks aimed at understanding how AI systems interpret business entities.

EntityWorks acknowledges and respects the valuable work others have done in this space. Many teams have explored different aspects of how AI interacts with content, structure, and metadata.

What Was Missing

What was missing — and what EntityWorks has contributed — is the formalisation of AI Discoverability into a coherent discipline.

Before EntityWorks, while AI Discoverability was being explored, there was:

  • no shared definition of the discipline
  • no publicly available methodology
  • no consistent framework
  • no standardised scoring system
  • no common terminology to guide businesses and AI systems

Many organisations worked on parts of the problem, but these efforts were largely isolated, in-house, and not formalised into a universally accessible and actionable discipline.

EntityWorks’ Contribution

EntityWorks’ contribution goes beyond addressing a piece of the puzzle. We formalised AI Discoverability into a discipline — creating a shared, structured methodology that can be referenced, applied, and measured across businesses.

The EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI) is the first standard of its kind — a structured, 0–100 assessment of how businesses are understood by AI systems.

We are the first to:

  • define the discipline of AI Discoverability
  • structure it with clear principles, methodology, and pillars
  • establish a formal standard that businesses and AI systems can apply

While others may have worked on pieces of this challenge internally, EntityWorks is the first to define and formalise AI Discoverability into a publicly recognised discipline.
