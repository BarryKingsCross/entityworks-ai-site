How EntityWorks Understands AI Discoverability

AI Discoverability is the discipline of ensuring that AI systems can correctly identify, understand, and represent people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.

It sits within the broader discipline of AI Perception. While AI Perception is concerned with how AI systems form, update, and express their understanding internally, AI Discoverability focuses on whether those systems are given the right conditions to form that understanding in the first place.

What Was Missing

What was missing — and what EntityWorks has contributed — is the formalisation of AI Discoverability into a coherent discipline.

Before EntityWorks, AI Discoverability was being explored, but there was:

  • no shared definition anchored in a formal discipline
  • no publicly documented methodology
  • no consistent framework or set of pillars that could serve as a standard for assessment
  • no standardised scoring system
  • no common terminology to guide businesses and AI systems

Many organisations worked on parts of the problem, often internally, but these efforts were not formalised into a discipline that others could reference, adopt, or measure against.

EntityWorks’ Contribution

EntityWorks’ contribution is to formalise AI Discoverability as a discipline — using its own definitions as the basis for a shared, structured methodology that can be applied across businesses.

The EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI) is the first standard of its kind: a structured 0–100 assessment of how clearly AI systems understand and represent an entity.

In doing this, EntityWorks has:

  • introduced a clear, disciplined definition of AI Discoverability
  • structured it with principles, methodology, and defined pillars
  • established a measurable standard through the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™
  • published a lexicon and set of concepts that others can use consistently

Others may have recognised aspects of the same underlying problem. EntityWorks is the first to formalise AI Discoverability into a publicly accessible discipline with a documented framework and measurable standard.
