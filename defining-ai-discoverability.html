What Is AI Discoverability?

AI Discoverability is the discipline of ensuring that AI systems can correctly identify, understand, and represent people, organisations, relationships, and ideas.

AI Discoverability focuses on how AI systems form and express their view of an entity. It examines the clarity, structure, and stability of the signals that models draw on to construct their internal understanding, and how reliably that understanding is expressed back to users.

Formalising an Undefined Space

AI Discoverability has appeared in scattered discussions and marketing copy, but it lacked a definition, a framework, or a formal structure. There was no shared language, no boundary, and no standard against which the concept could be assessed or measured.

EntityWorks formalised AI Discoverability into a discipline by:

  • defining its scope, purpose, and relationship to AI Perception
  • clarifying the concepts and language required for consistent interpretation
  • establishing a structured methodology and framework
  • introducing the first measurable standard through the EntityWorks Discoverability Index™
  • publishing the lexicon and principles underpinning the discipline

The EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI)

The EntityWorks Discoverability Index™ (EDI) is the first structured 0–100 framework for reviewing how clearly AI systems understand and represent an entity.

It converts an emerging, largely invisible problem into something measurable. The EDI includes:

  • a discoverability score from 0–100
  • a structured review across the core pillars of AI Discoverability
  • analysis of contradictions, drift, and fragmentation across signals
  • clear recommendations to strengthen AI-facing representation

The EDI is more than an assessment tool; it is the operational expression of AI Discoverability as a discipline.

Principles of the Discipline

AI Discoverability rests on several foundational principles:

  • Clarity — information must be interpretable without ambiguity.
  • Consistency — signals about an entity should align across sources and contexts.
  • Structure — data must be arranged in formats that AI systems can reliably interpret.
  • Stability — representation should remain coherent as models evolve and update.

These principles ensure that AI systems have the conditions required to form correct and durable representations — one of the core concerns of the broader discipline of AI Perception.
